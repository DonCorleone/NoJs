What is the tallest mountain on Earth?

Mount Everest, standing at 8,849 m above sea level. Its peak sits in the jet stream, where winds regularly exceed 200 km/h.

How do bees navigate without GPS?

By reading the sun's position and polarised light patterns in the sky — even on overcast days. They also communicate distance and direction through the waggle dance.

Why is the sky blue?

Rayleigh scattering. Shorter blue wavelengths bounce off air molecules far more than red ones, flooding the sky with blue light while reds travel straight through.

How long would it take to drive to the Moon?

At a steady 100 km/h it would take roughly 160 days — assuming the motorway extends that far and there are enough service stations along the way.

What makes a perfect espresso?

About 9 bars of pressure, 90–96 °C water, 25–30 seconds of extraction, and freshly ground beans within minutes of pulling the shot.

Why does coffee taste bitter sometimes?

Over-extraction — too fine a grind, too hot water, or too long a brew pulls bitter compounds out after all the good flavours are already gone.

What is the difference between arabica and robusta?

Arabica is smoother and more aromatic, grown at high altitude. Robusta is stronger, more bitter, higher in caffeine, and far hardier to grow.
